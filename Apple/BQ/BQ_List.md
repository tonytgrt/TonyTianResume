# Apple Interview – Behavioral Questions

Consolidated from multiple sources (phone screen reports, 48-question bank, example/reported questions).
Duplicates merged, irrelevant questions (market research, event planning, salary) removed.
Questions are sorted by theme. The most likely to appear in a 30–60 min SWE intern phone screen are marked **★**.

---

## 1. Self-Introduction & Background

- **★** Tell me about yourself. (education, experience, relevant skills)
- **★** Walk me through a coding project you worked on — what was your role and what technical challenge did you solve?
- What programming languages are you most comfortable with, and why?
- What have you been working on lately / what are you most excited about right now?

---

## 2. Why Apple

- **★** Why do you want to work at Apple? / Why Apple, and why this SWE Intern role?
- What is your favorite Apple product or service, and why?
- What do you know about Apple — its history, culture, products, and position in the industry?
- How does this role fit into your 5-year career or life plan?
- If we hired you, what would you want to work on?
- What do you think your greatest value to Apple would be?

---

## 3. Projects & Achievements

- **★** Describe a project you are most proud of — goals, challenges, and outcomes.
- Tell me about a project you were heavily involved in. What was your specific contribution?
- Describe the most challenging software development problem you faced. How did you solve it?
- Can you tell us about your single greatest professional or academic achievement, and why it was meaningful to you?

---

## 4. Handling Challenges & Failure

- **★** Tell me about a time you faced a significant challenge at work or in a project. How did you handle it?
- **★** Tell me about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly. How did you approach it?
- How have you overcome failure? What did you learn from it?
- Give an example of how you handled pressure and challenges — what would you do the same way at Apple?
- Describe your approach to solving complex problems — walk me through your thinking and steps.
- Tell me about a time a project was falling behind schedule. What did you do?

---

## 5. Teamwork & Conflict

- **★** Have you ever disagreed with a team member? How did you resolve it?
- Have you ever disagreed with a manager or supervisor about a decision? What happened and how did you handle it?
- Tell me about a time you played a positive role in driving a project forward within a team.
- Give an example of how you leveraged your strengths and compensated for others' weaknesses in a team.
- Tell me about a time you successfully persuaded others to accept your point of view.
- How do you handle a situation where you receive an unreasonable task or request?

---

## 6. Prioritization & Time Management

- **★** How do you prioritize tasks when working under pressure?
- If you were faced with multiple conflicting work tasks, what strategy would you use?
- How do you manage your time to complete work efficiently?
- If you faced an urgent and important task, how would you allocate your time and resources?

---

## 7. Growth & Self-Improvement

- Do you take steps to enhance your skills outside of class/work? Give an example.
- How do you keep learning and improving in your field?
- What are your personal strengths and weaknesses, and how do they affect your work?

---

## 8. Campus & Extracurricular (likely for students)

- Tell me about your campus or organization experiences. How have they shaped your professional skills?

---

## 9. Closing

- **★** Do you have any questions for me? (about the team, the work, the internship)

---

## Reported / Verbatim Questions from Past Interviews

These were directly reported by past candidates:

- "Why Apple, and why this SWE Intern role?"
- "Tell me about a coding project you worked on — what was your role and what technical challenge did you solve?"
- "What programming languages are you most comfortable with and why?"
- "Have you worked in a team before? Tell me about a time you encountered disagreement and how you resolved it."
- "What were some of your best and worst days over the last four years?"
- "What is the first thing you notice when walking into an Apple store?"
- "How would you test your favorite app?"

---

## Notes

- Apple strongly values **culture fit** — they want to see that you think like a craftsperson who cares deeply about the quality and details of what you build.
- Use **STAR format** for all behavioral answers: **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult.
- For a technical phone screen, expect ★-marked questions above plus 1–2 coding/technical questions.
- Since the job description is unknown, be ready to explain what you expect the UI Rendering GPU Software Team does and express genuine interest — Alex will fill in the details.
